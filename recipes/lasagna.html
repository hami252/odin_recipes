Lasagna

Description

Whenever I think about Lasagna it takes me back to Christmas eve as a child. Growing up it was my families tradition to invite friends over after Christmas eve service and enjoy a nice hot traditional lasagna. It was always the perfect dish for hosting. One pan, and doesn't require many sides besides a nice salad and some garlic bread. After dinner we would always get to open a single present before turning on A Christmas Story on TBS.

Today, I will be teaching you how to make a homemade lasagna, but with a twist. This lasagna will be prepared completely in a skillet. You are sure to be a fam favorite whem you break this dish out for dinner!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 pound ground beed
  • 28 ounces sphagetti sauce
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 2 teasponns dried basil
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cups dried malfada noodles
  • 1cup shredded mozzarella

Directions
  1. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook and stir beef in the hot skillet until browned and crumbly, 5 to 7 minutes. Drain and discard grease. Add spaghetti sauce, tomatoes, onion, garlic, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook over low heat until sauce is hot, about 15 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, fill a large pot with lightly salted water and bring to a rolling boil. Cook mafalda noodles at a boil until tender yet firm to the bite, about 8 minutes. Drain.
  3. Add cooked and drained noodles to the sauce and stir until completely coated. Sprinkle mozzarella cheese on top.
  4. Set an oven rack about 6 inches from the heat source and preheat the oven's broiler.
  5. Place skillet under the hot broil and cook until cheese is golden and bubbly, 3 to 5 minutes.
